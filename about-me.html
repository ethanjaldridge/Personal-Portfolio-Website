About Me

  Hello! My name is Ethan Aldridge. I'm a father, health enthusiast, author, guitarist, and an aspiring software developer. On this page, you'll find a bit about who I am as a person. The rest of this website is dedicated to my work in software. At the moment, I'm working my way through codecademy's full-stack course. I'm interested in how permaculture and blockchain technology can be used to improve the way we live. If you'd like to get in touch, you know what to do!

Health

  I have been strength training for over a decade, at an average of 4 times per week (though for a few years, it was everyday). That's over 2,000 workouts. I started working out by doing some pushups, curls, and situps in my bedroom a few days a week. Then I joined my high school football team, got some weights (for free!) and decided to stop football after the season, but keep strength training. Other than video games and drawing, strength training was the first thing that really interested me. I studied Elliott Hulse's stuff, then got into the "movement" world of Ido Portal. I got certified in Functional Range Conditioning (FRC), and studied flexibility from a handful of people: Jon Yuen, Emmet Louis, Kit Laughlin, and Jujimufu to name a few. I also picked up handbalancing thanks to Yuri Marmerstein's ebook, and taught myself to juggle, which just made sense because my Dad had always juggled for us as kids (I also learned to ride a skateboard for the same reason). I even learned how to do a backflip! After several years, I decided that I should compile what I had learned into a book. In doing so, I dove into bioenergetics and nutrition, where I learned from Weston Price, Ray Peat, Georgi Dinkov, and Gerald Pollack, again, just to name a few. I spent about ~1,500 hours over the course of a year writing my book. All this to say, I am quite passionate about health.

Music

  My earliest memory of music is probably my Dad, putting me to bed at my Grandparents' house to Sarah McLachlan's Surfacing. I also remember riding in his Acura Integra, listening to Coldplay, Incubus, and Jane's Addiction. I attempted to learn to play the guitar in 7th grade, but had little interest. In the summer before starting high school, my friend was learning bass. My step-mom at the time had an old 80's Ibanez, so I brought it over to his house one day. He showed me how to read tabs, and I started playing. I taught myself for a year, learning songs like Stellar by Incubus. After that first year, I started getting lessons from Danny Voris, who used to play at the bar my parent's worked at when they met. He taught me how to play with a pick (acoustic and electric guitar), until he ran out of things to teach me. Then, he taught me how to play classical guitar (using fingernails). Again, until he more or less ran out of things to teach me. As part of being his student, I got to perform at the church where he gave lessons, and also at The Greene, a local shopping center (once with my friends, and once for classical guitar). Now, I mostly play for my son, and for my own enjoyment. Danny, thank you for everything. You can find him here.

Art

  According to my parents, as soon as I could hold a crayon, I was always drawing. Eventually, at about the age of 14 my inner perfectionist got the best of me, and I had to put it down. I would stare at a blank piece of paper, not knowing what to draw. The creative reservoir dried up, so to speak. In retrospect, letting go of drawing (and video games) created the space that allowed me to learn to play the guitar, and to get into strength training. For anyone who is a creative type fighting the boredom to create, I would recommend exploring other outlets. Coding is my newest outlet, and it's bringing me a lot of enjoyment. Frustration, too... but I think it's a net positive :) Anyway, please enjoy some of my art!
